Pricing Strategies for Event Platforms

Revenue models ranked by fit for The Commons

★ Freemium + Transaction Fee (Recommended)

Free to create events and invite friends. Take a small percentage only when money flows through the platform (payment collection, escrow, vendor bookings).

Low barrier Viral growth Aligned incentives
Organiser fee: 0-3% on collected funds
Vendor marketplace: 10-15% commission on bookings
Who uses this: Partiful (free), Luma (free + 5%), Airbnb Experiences

Percentage-Based Commission

Charge a percentage of every transaction processed. Simple to understand, scales with event size. Risk: price sensitivity for large events.

Scales with GMV Price sensitive for big events
Typical range: 3.7-6.6% of ticket/payment value
Plus per-txn fee: $0.99-$1.79 per transaction
Who uses this: Eventbrite (3.7% + $1.79), Peatix (4.9% + $0.99)

Tiered Subscription

Monthly/annual plans unlocking features (analytics, branding, priority vendor access). Best for repeat organisers and corporate accounts.

Predictable revenue Harder cold-start
Typical tiers: Free / $25-59/mo / $99-199/mo / Enterprise
Reduces per-txn fee: Luma Plus ($59/mo) drops commission to 0%
Who uses this: Luma, Eventbrite (Pro), Splash

Vendor Marketplace Commission

Charge service providers (DJs, caterers, photographers) for leads and bookings. Organisers pay little or nothing -- vendors absorb platform cost.

Organiser-friendly B2B revenue Needs supply liquidity
Service marketplace standard: 15-20% of booking value
Lead fee model: $5-25 per qualified lead
Who uses this: Thumbtack (15-20%), GigSalad, Bark

Recommended Hybrid Model for The Commons

  • Free for organisers to create events and collect money from friends (drives adoption)
  • 2.5% platform fee on escrow/payment collection (covers payment processing margin)
  • 12-15% commission on vendor marketplace bookings (DJs, caterers, photographers)
  • Premium tier at S$19/mo for power organisers (0% payment fee, analytics, priority support)
  • Pass payment processing costs through transparently (PayNow ~0.4%, Cards ~3.4%)

Payment Strategy Recommendation

  • Default to PayNow for SG domestic (lowest fees, instant, universally adopted)

Key Price Sensitivity Drivers

  • • Event size matters: 2.5% on a S$50 collection (S$1.25) feels invisible; on S$10,000 (S$250) it feels steep
  • • Frequency effect: One-off organisers tolerate higher fees; repeat organisers want subscriptions
  • • Escrow premium: Users pay 1-3% more for guaranteed money protection vs raw transfers
  • • Comparison anchor: Eventbrite at ~10% makes anything under 5% feel like a deal
  • • Absorb vs pass-through: Organisers prefer "attendees pay the fee" option (Eventbrite model)

Platform Fee Benchmarks

  • • Eventbrite: ~10.2% effective rate (3.7% + 2.9% + $1.79)
  • • Peatix: ~6.9% effective rate (4.9% + $0.99)
  • • Luma: 5% + Stripe fees (~8-9% total)
  • • Partiful: 0% (not monetising yet, VC-subsidised)
  • • Splitwise: Free (expense tracking only, no collection)
  • • The Commons target: 2.5% platform + processing pass-through (~3-6% total)

Next Best Alternatives

What people currently use instead of The Commons

💬 WhatsApp Group + Bank Transfer

The #1 current solution in Singapore. Organiser creates a group, posts bank details, manually tracks who paid.

+ Free, everyone has it, instant
- Manual tracking, no escrow, organiser bears risk, messy for 15+ people

💲 Splitwise / Settle Up

Expense tracking after the fact. Great for splitting bills but does not collect money upfront or handle escrow.

+ Clean UX, well-known, tracks debts
- No upfront collection, no vendor booking, retrospective only

🎫 Eventbrite / Peatix

Ticketing for public/semi-public events. Overkill for private friend group events. High fees.

+ Established, reliable payments, discovery
- Not designed for P2P, no progressive payments, 7-10% fees

🎉 Partiful

Beautiful event invitations for casual social events. Free. But no payment collection, no vendor booking, US-focused.

+ Beautiful UX, viral, free, social features
- No payments, no escrow, no marketplace, US-centric

📝 Google Sheets + PayNow

DIY spreadsheet to track participants and payments. Common for organised friend groups and corporate events.

+ Free, customisable, familiar
- Very manual, no automation, error-prone, no reminders

📷 Instagram DMs + Direct Booking

For vendor discovery: browse Instagram, DM vendors directly, negotiate, pay via bank transfer.

+ Visual portfolios, direct relationship
- No price transparency, no escrow, no reviews verification

Regulatory Requirements

MAS licensing and compliance for escrow/payment handling in Singapore

Payment Services Act 2019 (PS Act)

The PS Act regulates payment service providers in Singapore. If The Commons holds or processes customer funds (escrow), it likely falls under regulated payment services and may need a license.

Requires Legal Review

MAS License Types

Standard Payment Institution (SPI): If monthly transaction volume is below S$3M. Major Payment Institution (MPI): If above S$3M/month. Escrow services may qualify as "e-money issuance" or "domestic money transfer".

Threshold-Based

Use a Licensed Partner (Shortcut)

Partner with a licensed payment processor (Stripe, HitPay, Airwallex) who already holds the MAS license. Funds flow through their licensed infrastructure -- The Commons acts as the marketplace layer, not a money transmitter.

Recommended Approach

Compliance Checklist

Requirement Status Action Needed
MAS PS Act licensing assessment Critical Engage legal counsel to determine if SPI or MPI license is needed, or if partner model exempts
Escrow fund segregation Critical Collected funds must be held in segregated trust accounts, not commingled with operating funds
AML/KYC requirements Important Know Your Customer checks for organisers collecting over threshold amounts
PDPA data protection Important Personal data consent, purpose limitation, data protection officer appointment
Consumer protection (refunds) Standard Clear refund policy, dispute resolution process, T&C for escrow release conditions
GST registration Standard Required if annual taxable turnover exceeds S$1M; platform commissions are GST-able

Key Strategic Takeaways

1. Win on free
Be free where Eventbrite/Peatix charge. The payment collection feature alone replaces WhatsApp + bank transfer chaos. Viral loop through payment links.
2. Monetise the marketplace
Vendor commissions (12-15%) are where the real margin lives. Organisers bring demand; vendors pay for access. Escrow justifies the fee.
3. PayNow-first in SG
Lowest fees, instant settlement, universal adoption. Makes the 2.5% platform fee palatable when processing cost is near-zero.
4. Regulatory via partner
Use Stripe/HitPay as licensed payment infrastructure. Avoids MAS licensing at launch. Revisit own license when GMV exceeds S$3M/month.
